About the Online Tutorial on Academic Integrity

How to use this tutorial

This tutorial is designed to work in Java-enabled Internet Explorer browser and is best viewed in 800x600 resolution. For the tutorial to work properly, pop-up blockers should be disabled and other software running on the local computer should not interfere with the tutorial. Do not open the tutorial by clicking a link for it through any other software such as Blackboard, Word or other documents, but type the tutorial URL directly on the browser and open the tutorial. This is to prevent java code from other programs interfering with the tutorial. The tutorial requires at least a Pentium 4 class personal computer with 512 MB of RAM.

Do not close the tutorial content screens when taking quizzes. Your quiz scores are temporarily held on the tutorial screens until you print the certificate and if you close the tutorial content screens and try to print the certificate, the quizzes may hang up and you may not be able to print the scores and the certificate of completion. No personal information or quiz scores are permanently saved on the tutorial site.

This tutorial is intended for self-paced learning by students in all disciplines. It is recommended that students review the content sections in the order they are presented and complete the activities included with each section in one session. However, if students cannot complete the content and activities in one session, then they are recommended to review the content in one or more sessions and then complete the quizzes in one session so that they can print the Certificate of Completion. Completion of games or cases is not required for obtaining the Certificate of Completion.

The content and activities (except the games) of this tutorial have been designed to be accessible to users' with disabilities. Students with disabilities who would like a transcript of the games can email facdev@niu.edu to request it. Students with particular disabilities that prevent them from reviewing the tutorial or completing the quizzes are encouraged to contact their instructors for reasonable accommodations.

Instructors can use the tutorial as part of their classroom discussions on academic integrity and encourage students to review the content and complete the activities as part of a course activity. But instructors are recommended to review the tutorial fully and complete the quizzes before assigning the tutorial as a course activity to students so that instructors can make sure the tutorial works properly on local computers and browsers.

The tutorial should neither be considered as a comprehensive resource on academic integrity nor should be used to test students on academic integrity. The Certificate of Completion that students can print after completing the tutorial successfully is only a record of their completion, and it cannot authenticate individual students actually reviewing the content and completing the quizzes successfully.

It should be emphasized here that the tutorial is intended only to promote academic integrity, and not to discuss university policies and procedures on academic dishonesty or discuss writing strategies. Faculty are recommended to cover university policies and procedures in their course syllabi, discuss good writing strategies in the classroom, and refer students to appropriate resources on campus and on the Web for more information.
